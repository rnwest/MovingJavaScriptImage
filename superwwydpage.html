What Would You Do?

Iron Man is flying over New York when he sees the Green Goblin throwing pumpkin bombs at the Empire State Building! Now, he knows that the Green Goblin is really Spider-Man's problem, but he can't see Spider-Man anywhere! If you were Iron Man, would you stop Green Goblin, even though he isn't YOUR problem? Would you take responsibility?

YES   NO

The correct answer is YES. Even though Green Goblin is really Spider-Man's archnemesis, as a fellow superhero, it is Iron Man's responsibility to the people he protects to stop the villain! Just because something might be someone else's responsibility, doesn't mean you shouldn't help out--especially if that person isn't there or can't take care of it.

Spider-Man wakes up one morning and realizes that, holy cow! He's in the Venom suit again! Now, Spider-Man knows that being in the venom suit makes him more powerful--in fact, it feels amazing to fight crime in, but it also makes him be mean to everyone around him. In the suit, he feels strong and can probably fight crime better, but without it, he's a nicer person who fights with his heart just as much as he does with his fists. If you were Spider-Man, would you take the suit off, even if it meant you would be weak physically? Would you sacrifice power to be a better person?

YES   NO

Despite being physically stronger in the suit, Spider-Man becomes mean--almost villainous when he wears it. Using it goes against everything it means to be a hero. In life there will be many times where something feels good to you, but makes you a bad person. If you care about other people, it's your responsibility not to give in and let your vices ruin the hero in you!

Daredevil is in a super secret lab. He has been investigating some mysterious technology used by his enemies that causes people to become mindless zombies! The trail has lead him here, and he sees what might be the technology not far away. However, he's not sure, and there's no one around to interrogate--no one to defend themselves. He doesn't want more people to become zombies, but what if someone led him here in the hopes that he would destroy everything? What if that's not the technology he was looking for? If you were Daredevil, would you leave the lab and investigate more? If so, click yes. Or would you destroy whatever is there in the hopes that you solved the problem? If so, click no.

YES   NO

It's not fair to accuse people when they're not there to defend themselves--especially when you're not sure of what the situation is. Daredevil does investigate more, and he finds out that his enemies led him to that lab so he would destroy it, because the scientists in that lab were working on a machine to cure the zombies! If he had acted on a false asumption, he would've condemed his city to becoming zombies.
